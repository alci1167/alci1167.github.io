003 - Project 1: Wood Polygon

I had a very ambitious idea for this project. I initially wanted to do a truncated icosahedron which is the same shape that a soccer ball has. The shape consists of 12 pentagons and 20 hexagons. A quick Wikipedia search told me the angles needed to put this pieces together. This ended up neing the reason why I could not complete the truncated icosahedron.

I started the project by spliting my pieces of maple and pine wood in half on the table saw.

I then laser cut a pentagon and hexagon from acrylic to use as a template to draw on top of the wood.

Using these outlines, I cut the pieces out with the band saw. I tried to leave a good amount of space between where I cut and the shapes so I could sand them down to the edges.

After sanding them and getting the basic shape down I made a "jig" to cut out the angles from both the hexagons and pentagons. This did not end up working very well. I was unable to make a "jig" that helped all shapes have the appropriate angles. Since I was unable to do the cuts accurately, I could not make the truncated icosahedron. I now had a bunch of hexagons and pentagons and had to come up with something else.

I ended up staking the hexagons in groups of three and gluing them together. I then sanded the sides so the could sit flush next to eachother. I then glued the pentagons in between these stacks.

The final couple steps involved sanding. I could have done a better job at this, but I was honestly tired and ready to finish the piece. I finished this piece with linseed oil.

Overall the project was challenging and I learned a lot of things. There are many thing I would do differently, but I think this was a good experience. I am proud of my work and hope to improve as I move along with this class.
