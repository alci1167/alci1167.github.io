003 - Project 1: Wood Polygon

I had a very ambitious idea for this project. I initially wanted to do a truncated icosahedron which is the same shape that a soccer ball has. The shape consists of 12 pentagons and 20 hexagons. A quick Wikipedia search told me the angles needed to put this pieces together. This ended up neing the reason why I could not complete the truncated icosahedron.

I started the project by spliting my pieces of maple and pine wood in half on the table saw.

I then laser cut a pentagon and hexagon from acrylic to use as a template to draw on top of the wood.

Using these outlines, I cut the pieces out with the band saw. I tried to leave a good amount of space between where I cut and the shapes so I could sand them down to the edges.
