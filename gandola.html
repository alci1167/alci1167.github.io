004 - Project 3: Plastic gandola

I used HDPE and PETE plastic for my gandola. I got a PETE container of apple-pears that had three slots and seemed perfect for the project. I also used a PETE plastic that came from Chromebook screens. I used PETE water bottles and PE-LD(LDPE) bags from the Chromebook screens.

After having all the materials, I made a sketch of what I had in mind. All I honestly knew was that the apple-pear container was going to be the main structure.

I cleaned the plastic materials and was ready to work on the gandola.

I wedged in the water bottles into the contaier by cutting into the middle and making cuts around in a circle.

I cut the bottles in half and stuffed the ends into the top half of the water bottles. I also used the other half of the apple-pear container and cut slits into it to be able to easily place the eggs.

I then poked holes on the top of the container and used strips of the bag to make a hinge. I used the blue plastic bags and PETE plastic to stuff the containers. To hang on the wire I made a handle by poking holes and using the a strip of PETE plastic as the handle.

The final product seems pretty solid and should safely hold all three eggs.
