The remote controlled car was developed as a team project for "Form" at ATLS. To create a more interactive experience, we also developed a laser gate and display to track lap times.

The car had 4 motors individually controlled by a motor shield we connected to the Arduino Uno. The Arduino also had a power source with a switch and a reciever. All of this was hidden under the body of the car that we 3D printed. To go with the reciever we created a controller that used another Arduino, a battery, a joystic, and a transmitter. Those components were protected under a case made of acrylic.

A large portion of the project was interactivity. The laser gate added a another element to the RC car. We used another Arduino Uno, a display, and a laser to time the users lap.

You can find more about this project on our Instructables page.
